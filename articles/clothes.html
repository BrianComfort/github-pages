Where brands get their clothes

Brands like Tillys typically order clothes from the manufacture itself. An example can be billabong.com or addias.com

Cost To Entry

Don't get me wrong, I think you can easily lose money doing this. However, that doesn't mean you can't get any either. In a worse case scenario, clothes don't sell and that's that, now you have stuff to sell on Facebook Marketplace.

But to actaully answer the question, I think it truly depends on how much supply and marketing you need. At the end of the day, online ecommerce is soley dependant on finding buyers which is often online.

I'd set 500 dollars for this. 150 on clothes, and the rest on marketing. Since I know how to build ecommece sites, building it is easy. I also have a lot of storage to hold all the clothes.

Of course, maybe I'm wrong. For the future reader, let me know!

How Clothing Brands Market

With a little bit of reaserch, I found that most brands like Tillys and PacSun market in two diffrent ways:

  1. Models show off shirts

The caveat to this is that models are expensive and to make it profitiable, we'll need a lot of shirts so they can wear a lot at the same time. Also, you need a lot of models for it to work.

  1. Just showing the photo of the clothing piece against a solid background (mostly white)

I think this is perfectly fine for starting out, but does require a camrea (phone) and some white lights to get it perfect. When taking photos, we take it from multipe angles so when the user goes to incpect the article of clothing, they have multiple shots of it.

Now both of these can be used for advertising purposes, and I think the first method is more efective in customer accusition. Again, the second is good to start out with.

Maybe this is a lesson in buiness school but I think marketing is done in two diffrent ways as well:

  1. Direct
  2. Indirect

By direct, I mean the photos themselves, the advertisements etc. By indirect, I say the brand itself, the logos the colors the instagram accounts.

That being said, brand does mean a lot in the long run and such I think should be considered.

Web Application

The webstack I'm going to use is the one I'm most comfortable with which is Flask and AWS. I'll use a single ec2 instance to start as almost all customers are going to be from the U.S and canada. Using a single instance makes it a lot easier to figure out databases as well as load blanacning. I should explore other options but this is most likely going to be the best solution to my problems.

Databases

I'm not the best at databases but what I need is to have a database with order Id's and the fuffilment of id's. I'm thinking a traditional postgres or mysql would be the best option for this.

This means on the websever, I'll need to have a function that can create and write those ids and as for the "real world", I should make a python program for myself so when an order is fuffiled, I can send an email, fuffil the order id, and then write to a log book the reveune, profit etc.

Also, I'll need a database with every indviual product, their accomnping photos (like the photo of the back, the front the side), the size of the shirt, the color, the brand etc. Even better, I should make the database include number purchased, left in stock, as well as the amount of clicks each shirt recives.
